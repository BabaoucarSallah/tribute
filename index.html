Tribute to Biri Biri

Biri Biri (Alhaji Momodou Njie) was a Gambian legend, widely regarded as the greatest footballer The Gambia has ever produced. His skill, speed, and goal-scoring ability made him a national icon and an inspiration for future generations.

Milestones in Biri Biri’s career include:

  • Biri biri was born at Perseverance Street Banjul in 1948.
  • attended the Crab Island Junior Secondary where his talents were first discovered in the Inter-School Soccer competitions when Biri dominated Junior Soccer and brought many honours to his school.
  • Biri Biri played for several clubs in The Gambia and Sierra Leone.
    • Black Star Football Club
    • Black Diamonds
    • White Phantoms
    • Arrance FC
    • 1963–1965 Mighty Blackpool - Sierra Leone
    • 1965–1970 Augustinians
  • He Became the first Gambian to play professionally in Europe.
  • In 1970 , Biri left The Gambia to join Derby County, a first division team in UK. He returned to The Gambia in 1970 and join Wallidan a team he actually showed a lot of love for and attachment.
  • 1972, Biri went to Denmark to play for B1901, a first division team in that country.
  • 1973 he was transferred to that famous Spanish team Sevilla FC
    • Playing for Sevilla FC in Spain from 1973 to 1978, where he became a fan favorite.

      He scored 34 goals in 109 matches for the club, and was a vital player in the promotion-winning side in the 74/75 season

    • Winning the Copa del Generalísimo (now Copa del Rey) with Sevilla.
  • Representing The Gambia at the international level and leaving a lasting legacy in African football.

Even after his retirement, Biri Biri remained a beloved figure in Gambian sports and was honored for his contributions to football. His legacy continues to inspire young athletes across the country.
